Austin

Austin is the colorful state capital of Texas. With the city slogan “Keep Austin Weird”, it’s no wonder that the city boasts an eclectic live music, art and food scene. Home to the University of Texas, Austin hosts several popular cultural festivals including Austin City Limits and SXSW. On any given day, you’ll catch Austin-ites enjoying Texas barbecue, kayaking the Colorado River or taking in a concert.

Boston

Boston intersects the past and the future with a perfect mixture of historically charming neighborhoods and a bustling urban downtown. Being the capital of Massachusetts and the largest city in New England, Boston is a hub for culture, academics and history. Founded in 1630, the city is situated on Boston Harbor and is famous for institutions like Fenway Park, the Freedom Trail and Harvard University.

Chicago

Nestled along the shores of Lake Michigan is the culturally-rich city of Chicago. The midwestern metropolis boasts an impressive skyline, dozens of museums and a dining scene that is consistently ranked among the best in the world. The city is known for a sprawling live blues and jazz scene, as well as its 77 unique neighborhoods. Popular Chicago destinations include The Field Museum, Navy Pier and Wrigley Field.

Los Angeles

Los Angeles, the second largest city in the US, is known for its laid back vibe, beautiful weather and, of course, Hollywood. With its abundance of theatres, movie studios and celebrities, LA has been dubbed “The Entertainment Capital of the World.” Need a break from the glitz and glam of downtown? Los Angeles’ neighboring cities feature 15 beaches and extensive hiking trails in the San Gabriel Mountains.

New York City

New York City is a global icon on the forefront of culture, finance and media. As the most populous city in the US, NYC features world-class institutions like The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Wall Street, the Statue of Liberty and the Empire State Building. The city is comprised of five different boroughs; each with their own unique cultural, food and architecture scenes. NYC surely offers something for everyone.

San Francisco

Every neighbohood in San Francisco has its own personality, from the hippie chic of the Upper Haight to the hipster grit of the Mission. The Marina district boasts trendy bistros and postcard-perfect views of the Golden Gate Bridge, while Noe Valley offers quaint and quiet boutiques. Wave hello to the sea lions at Pier 39, and sample local cheese and charcuterie at the Ferry Building. Sit in on a yoga session in Dolores Park or marvel at the Dutch Windmill across from Ocean Beach.

Seattle

Tucked away in the Pacific Northwest is the sprawling eco-friendly, tech-focused city of Seattle. The city’s temperate climate makes it the ideal year-round destination for outdoors lovers. Seattle is nicknamed “The Emerald City” for the National Parks and lush, green forests that surround it. Additionally, Seattle is well-known for its coffee, the Space Needle, Pike Place Market and a lively music scene.
